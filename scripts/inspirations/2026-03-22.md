### Poem of the Day

> #### Watching the Sea Go
> **Dana Levin**
>   
> &emsp;Thirty seconds of yellow lichen.  
> 
> Thirty seconds of coil and surge,  
> &emsp;fern and froth, thirty seconds  
> &emsp;&emsp;of salt, rock, fog, spray.  
> 
> &emsp;&emsp;&emsp;&emsp;&emsp;Clouds  
> 
> moving slowly to the left—  
> 
> &emsp;A door in a rock through which you could see  
> 
> &emsp;&emsp;&emsp;__  
> 
> another rock,  
> &emsp;&emsp;laved by the weedy tide.  
> 
> &emsp;Like filming breathing—thirty seconds  
> 
> of tidal drag, fingering  
> &emsp;the smaller stones  
> &emsp;&emsp;down the black beach—what color  
> 
> &emsp;was that, aquamarine?  
> Starfish spread  
> 
> &emsp;&emsp;their salmon-colored hands.  
> 
> &emsp;&emsp;&emsp;__  
> 
> 
> &emsp;I stood and I shot them.  
>   
> I stood and I watched them  
> &emsp;right after I shot them: thirty seconds of smashed sea  
> &emsp;&emsp;while the real sea  
> 
> &emsp;&emsp;thrashed and heaved—  
> 
> &emsp;They were the most boring movies ever made.  
> I wanted  
> 
> &emsp;&emsp;to mount them together and press Play.  
> 
> &emsp;&emsp;&emsp;__  
> 
> 
> &emsp;Thirty seconds of waves colliding.  
> Kelp  
> 
> &emsp;with its open attitudes, seals  
> &emsp;&emsp;riding the swells, curved in a row  
> 
> &emsp;&emsp;just under the water—  
> 
> &emsp;&emsp;&emsp;the sea,  
> 
> &emsp;over and over.  
> &emsp;&emsp;&emsp;Before it's over.
