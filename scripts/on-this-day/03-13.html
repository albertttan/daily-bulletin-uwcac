March 13

  • 1741 – War of Jenkins' Ear: The British began an assault against Spanish forts in the Caribbean in the Battle of Cartagena de Indias (depicted).
  • 1848 – Conservative statesman, Klemens von Metternich, is forced to resign as the foreign minister of Austria following student demonstrations in Vienna.
  • 1964 – Kitty Genovese was murdered in New York City, prompting research into the bystander effect due to the false story that neighbors witnessed the killing and did nothing to help her.
  • 1996 – A mass shooting at a primary school occurred in Dunblane, Scotland, killing 16 children and a teacher and prompting tighter gun control in the UK.

Births and Deaths: John Griffin, 4th Baron Howard de Walden (b. 1719); Adolf Anderssen (d. 1879); Meinhard Michael Moser (b. 1924); Jan Howard (b. 1929)
